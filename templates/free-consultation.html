FAQ

Common Questions

Is this really free? No hidden costs?
Yes, it is genuinely free. There is no obligation to buy anything, no credit card required, and no follow-up pressure. We offer this because the best client relationships start with honest conversation — and that begins with understanding your context before talking about budgets or proposals.
What happens after the call? Will I be contacted repeatedly?
No. You will receive a written summary of our discussion and recommendations. That is it. We do not have a sales team, automated email sequences, or follow-up campaigns. If you want to continue the conversation, you are welcome to reach out. If not, we wish you well.
What kind of questions can I ask?
Anything related to AI, technology strategy, or your specific project. Common topics include: whether AI makes sense for your business, what kind of solution fits your needs, build-vs-buy decisions, technology stack recommendations, cost estimates, timeline guidance, architecture validation, and governance or compliance considerations. If we cannot answer your question, we will tell you honestly and point you to someone who can.
Who will I speak with?
You will speak directly with Jonathan, the founder and lead consultant. He has 20+ years of experience building production systems — from multiplayer game engines and enterprise Drupal platforms to blockchain indexers and AI agent systems. He works hands-on with every client and does not outsource consultations.
What if I need more than 30 minutes?
If it is clear that your situation requires a deeper engagement, we will be upfront about it during the call and outline what a paid engagement would look like — scope, timeline, and cost. There is no pressure to commit on the call. The free consultation is designed to help you make an informed decision about whether and how to proceed.
Do you offer this in Vietnamese?
Yes. Jonathan has been based in Da Nang, Vietnam since 2015 and works regularly with Vietnamese-speaking clients. The consultation can be conducted in Vietnamese or English — whichever you prefer.

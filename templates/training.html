FAQ

Common Questions

How is training different from consulting?
Consulting solves a specific business problem for you — we build a system, design an architecture, or deliver a strategy. Training builds your team's own capability so they can solve problems independently. Both are valuable, but they serve different needs. Many clients start with consulting to establish direction, then use training to embed skills across their teams. We can help you determine the right mix during a discovery conversation.
How much does a training programme cost?
It depends on format, duration, and level of customisation. Half-day workshops start at $3,000–5,000. Multi-day in-person bootcamps range from $7,000–15,000. Multi-week cohort programs and custom enterprise engagements are scoped individually. We provide a detailed proposal with fixed pricing after an initial scoping call.
Do you customise content for our industry?
Yes. Every program is adapted to your industry context, regulatory environment, and operational realities. We use your actual workflows, data examples, and tooling during exercises. Generic training is far less effective — we never deliver a one-size-fits-all curriculum.
What if we need to train a large organisation?
We run enterprise-scale programmes for organisations with 500+ employees using a train-the-trainer model. We equip your internal L&D team with curriculum materials, facilitator guides, and coaching so they can scale training across departments independently. We remain available for advanced sessions and ongoing support.
Do participants receive certification?
Yes. Participants receive a certificate of completion with CEU credits where applicable. For custom programs, we include validated skill assessments and can integrate with your internal competency frameworks. Completion rates for our in-person programmes exceed 95%.
How do you measure training effectiveness?
We measure at four levels: participant satisfaction (reaction), skill gain through pre/post assessments (learning), application in daily work (behaviour), and business impact — productivity gains, automation wins, and governance improvements (results). We provide a detailed impact report after each programme.

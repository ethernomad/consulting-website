Overview

Why Adoption Fails

Most organisations treat AI adoption as a technical implementation. They build the system, deploy it, and expect people to start using it. That almost never works. New tools create uncertainty, change existing workflows, and challenge established habits — and without deliberate support, teams default back to what they know.

Change management closes that gap. It is the structured practice of preparing, enabling, and reinforcing new behaviours until they stick. Applied to AI, it means treating adoption as a first-class workstream — with the same rigour, resourcing, and attention as the technical build.

Prepare

Assess readiness, align leadership, identify champions, and address resistance before launch so the organisation is ready to adopt

Enable

Equip every stakeholder with the skills, documentation, and support they need to use the AI system confidently in their daily work

Reinforce

Sustain adoption through office hours, champions, pulse checks, and continuous improvement loops that adapt as workflows evolve

Common challenges

Why AI Adoption Stalls

We see the same patterns across organisations. Recognising them early is the first step to avoiding them.

No leadership alignment

Executives approve the budget but don't model usage. Teams follow what leaders prioritise — if leaders don't use the AI system, neither will the teams.

No user involvement in design

Systems built without end-user input produce workflows that don't match how people actually work. The result: workarounds, shadow processes, and eventual abandonment.

Inadequate training

Generic, one-size-fits-all training that doesn't account for different roles, skill levels, or workflows. People don't know how to apply the tool to their actual job, so they don't bother.

No post-launch support

Training happens before go-live, but questions and friction emerge after. Without ongoing support, small frustrations compound into full abandonment within weeks.

Cultural resistance

Fear of job displacement, distrust of AI decisions, or simple fatigue from constant tool changes. These concerns are rational and need to be addressed directly, not ignored.

No adoption measurement

If you aren't tracking adoption metrics, you won't know you have a problem until the system has already failed. Early warning signs are invisible without deliberate measurement.

FAQ

Common Questions

How is change management different from training?
Training builds capability — it teaches someone how to use a tool. Change management builds willingness and habit — it addresses fear, uncertainty, workflow disruption, and the organisational dynamics that prevent adoption. Both are necessary, but they serve different purposes. We often deliver change management as a complement to technical training, ensuring the skills taught in the classroom actually stick in daily work.
When should we start adoption planning?
As early as possible. Ideal timing is during the AI strategy or pilot phase — before the system is built. This allows the adoption plan to influence system design (workflows, UX, rollout sequencing) rather than retrofitting adoption after launch. For existing systems that have already stalled, we can do a rapid adoption assessment and design a recovery plan within 2-3 weeks.
How do you handle resistance from teams?
Resistance is almost always a signal, not a problem. It usually means someone's concerns haven't been heard, their workflow hasn't been considered, or they lack the support to make the transition. We surface resistance early through stakeholder interviews and pulse surveys, address the underlying causes directly, and work with champions to turn skeptics into advocates. Most resistance dissolves when people feel heard and equipped.
How long does an adoption programme run?
A typical engagement spans 8-16 weeks: 2-4 weeks of assessment and planning, 2-4 weeks of preparation and workshop delivery, and 4-8 weeks of post-launch office hours and reinforcement. We structure the programme so internal champions can sustain adoption independently after the engagement ends. For smaller rollouts, we offer a scaled-down 4-6 week programme.
Do you work with existing AI systems that aren't being adopted?
Yes. We frequently help organisations recover stalled AI investments. We do a rapid adoption diagnostic to understand why the system isn't being used, identify the blockers, and design a targeted intervention — which might include refresher workshops, improved documentation, champion reactivation, workflow adjustments, or leadership alignment sessions.
How do you measure adoption success?
We track a combination of leading and lagging indicators: active usage rates, feature adoption depth, task completion efficiency, user satisfaction (via pulse surveys), support ticket volume, and business impact metrics tied to the system's original ROI case. We establish baseline measurements before launch and report progress weekly during the engagement, then transition reporting to your internal team.

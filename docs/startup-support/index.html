Overview

What is Startup Support?

Startup and innovation support is purpose-built for early-stage ventures and corporate innovation teams that need speed, experimentation, and product thinking — not large-scale enterprise transformation. Whether you are a first-time founder validating a thesis, an incubator preparing portfolio companies for demo day, or a corporate team launching a new venture, the goal is the same: get from idea to evidence as fast as possible.

We combine deep AI engineering in Rust with product discovery and lean startup methodologies. The result is a structured path from concept to funded, production-ready product — with working prototypes, real user feedback, and investor-grade demonstrations at every milestone.

Speed

Go from idea to working prototype in weeks, not months — with focused sprints that respect your runway and timeline

Validation

Test assumptions with real users and real data before committing to full-scale development or raising your next round

Scale

Build on production-grade Rust foundations that scale from MVP to million-user service without rewrites

Our process

How We Help You Build

We take you from concept to validated, investor-ready product — with working software at every stage, not promises. Each step produces tangible output you can use, test, and show.

  1. Step 1
    ●

    Discovery Sprint

    Map your market, users, and technical approach. Identify the riskiest assumption and the one feature that will prove or disprove it. Define success criteria, prioritise the roadmap, and produce a build plan with timeline and budget.

  2. Step 2
    ●

    Design & Prototype

    Design the system architecture, data model, and user experience. Build a working prototype with real data to validate technical feasibility and user response. Test with target users, gather feedback, and iterate before committing to the full build.

  3. Step 3
    ●

    Build & Validate

    Develop the MVP with your core feature set — built in Rust for performance, reliability, and single-binary deployment. Deploy to a live environment, onboard early users, and measure against the success criteria defined in discovery. Iterate based on real usage signals.

  4. Step 4
    ●

    Launch & Iterate

    Present your product to investors, accelerator demo days, or early customers with a polished demo system. Establish feedback loops, plan the next iteration sprint, and hand over documentation and architecture for your team to own. Ongoing iteration sprints available as needed.

FAQ

Common Questions

How is Startup Support different from AI Strategy or App Building?
AI Strategy is about deciding what to build and why — identifying opportunities and building a roadmap. App Building is about developing full-production cross-platform applications. Startup Support sits between them: it is the rapid validation and execution layer for early-stage ventures. We help you move from hypothesis to working prototype in weeks, with the explicit goal of getting to investment, first customers, or a go/pivot/kill decision. Many clients start with AI Strategy, move into Startup Support for rapid validation, and then graduate to App Building for production scale.
How fast can you go from idea to working prototype?
A focused prototype can be built in 2–4 weeks, depending on scope and data readiness. A complete MVP with a single core feature, user interface, and investor-ready demo typically takes 4–8 weeks. The key constraint is usually data — if your market or technology requires proprietary data that needs to be sourced or cleaned, that adds time. We start every engagement with a Discovery Sprint to scope the work accurately before committing to a timeline.
What does a typical engagement cost?
We structure engagements as fixed-scope sprints rather than open-ended time-and-materials. A Discovery Sprint starts from $5,000. A focused MVP with AI features and investor demo typically ranges from $25,000 to $80,000, depending on complexity and the number of iteration sprints required. We provide a fixed-price quote after the Discovery Sprint, so you know the full cost before committing to the build phase.
Do I need a technical co-founder to work with you?
Not at all. Many of our startup clients are non-technical founders who need a technical partner to build their first product, prepare for fundraising, and attract technical talent. We act as your technical team during the critical early stages — scoping, building, and validating — while you focus on customers, market fit, and raising capital. When you are ready to hire, we provide knowledge transfer and architecture documentation for a smooth handoff.
What happens after the MVP is built?
Most clients continue with iteration sprints — 2–4 week cycles that add features, improve UX, and respond to early user feedback. Others use the MVP to close their funding round and then transition to an in-house team with our knowledge transfer. Some commission a full production build through our App Building service. We recommend a path based on your stage, runway, and goals during the Discovery Sprint, so there are no surprises.
Can you work with my existing codebase or team?
Yes. We integrate with your existing codebase, APIs, and infrastructure — whether it is in Rust, Python, TypeScript, or another language. We also work alongside your existing engineering team, providing specific expertise in AI, Rust, or product architecture that your team may not have in-house. Our goal is to augment your capabilities, not replace them.

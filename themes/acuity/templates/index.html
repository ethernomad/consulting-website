Why legacy wikis fall short

The dominant wiki model has structural limits.

Acuity is designed around the weaknesses of centralized encyclopedias: rigid neutrality norms, missing provenance, fragile hosting, and narrow control over who can participate and innovate.

Neutrality is limited in practice

Wikipedia often presents itself as an authority of truth instead of acknowledging that many topics contain a range of legitimate interpretations. Views outside the prevailing Overton window can struggle to persist, while revision wars and alternative forks are weak mechanisms for reaching durable consensus.

Notability rules leave real gaps

Many worthwhile subjects are excluded because they are too small, too local, or insufficiently covered by established institutions, leaving valid knowledge out of the public record.

Knowledge stays downstream of existing sources

Wikipedia depends on already-published material. It organizes knowledge, but does not provide a framework for original research, direct evidence trails, or new knowledge formation inside the network itself.

Trust is centralized

The system ultimately depends on databases and infrastructure controlled by a single foundation. There is no collaborative hosting model at the protocol level, no cryptographic proof of who did what when, and no shared base layer for independent operators.

Availability and censorship remain risks

A centralized service can go down, degrade, or become inaccessible. The same chokepoints also make the platform more susceptible to legal, political, and institutional censorship pressure.

Innovation is permissioned at the platform layer

The backend is not fully decoupled from the frontend, so only the foundation has complete ability to innovate across the full system. Everyone else builds at the edges rather than on equal footing.

How Acuity works

One article does not have to mean one official version.

Acuity Wiki treats knowledge more like open-source software than a single locked page. Articles are namespaced by author, edits to someone else's work create forks, and links between articles help the network discover which branches are most trusted.

Wikipedia

One page competing for control

  • One canonical article tries to represent the final consensus.
  • Editors push changes into the same page and fight over what stays.
  • Disagreement is usually settled by reversion, policy, or administrator process.
  • Links point to one approved article rather than competing interpretations.

Acuity Wiki

A network of article branches

  • Every article lives in an author namespace such as @alice/Quantum_Mechanics.
  • Editing someone else's article creates your fork, like a GitHub project.
  • You can merge upstream changes, propose improvements back upstream, or maintain a divergent branch.
  • Other articles choose which fork to link to, creating visible trust and popularity signals.

Software stack

A modular stack for verifiable, scalable wiki infrastructure.

Acuity Wiki combines Polkadot security, a dedicated parachain, on-chain content events, decentralized storage, and a cross-platform client to create an open knowledge network with verifiable provenance.

Polkadot Relay Chain

  • Provides shared security and direct access to Hydration DEX.

Acuity Wiki Parachain

  • All writes to the wiki are transactions on this chain.
  • Provides massive transaction capacity and low fees.
  • Native coin ACW is tradeable on Hydration.

Content Pallet

  • Lightweight Substrate pallet.
  • Emits an IPFS CID for each article revision.
  • Already written by Jonathan Brown.

Acuity Index

  • A Polkadot event index.
  • Indexes events from the Content Pallet.
  • Query results contain finality proofs.
  • Funded by two Web3 Foundation grants, including a second follow-on grant, and a Kusama Treasury referendum.

IPFS

  • Provides a shared filesystem for users to share their content.
  • Content can be pinned by dedicated services subscribed to the Content Pallet.

Acuity Wiki Dapp

  • Written in the Dioxus Rust framework.
  • Targets Web, macOS, Windows, iOS, and Android.
  • Includes a light client to verify event proofs.
  • Forked from the existing acuity-dioxus codebase.

Why incentives are misaligned

Wikipedia is not built to discover reality.

Its incentive structure is strongest at preserving acceptable consensus, citing approved institutions, and resolving disputes procedurally. It is much weaker at uncovering overlooked truths, integrating direct evidence, or rewarding contributors for being right before a view becomes mainstream.

Consensus is rewarded over truth

Editors are rewarded for producing text that survives community review and policy enforcement, not for discovering what is most accurate. What remains is often what is most acceptable to the editing culture.

Original discovery is structurally blocked

Because original research is discouraged, contributors cannot use the platform to test claims, surface new evidence, or build knowledge from first principles. The system follows reality only after others have already documented it.

Institutional approval outranks independent judgment

Claims gain legitimacy mainly when they are backed by recognized sources. This gives existing institutions disproportionate power even when they are incomplete, biased, slow to update, or simply wrong.

Conflict resolution replaces epistemic progress

When editors disagree, the platform tends to reward compromise, reversion, and policy argument rather than rigorous methods for determining what is true. Debate becomes procedural instead of truth-seeking.

Accurate minority views struggle to survive

Views outside the mainstream are often filtered out unless they already have broad recognition. That makes it difficult for emerging, heterodox, or unpopular truths to gain durable representation.

There is no reward for being right early

The system offers little incentive to investigate overlooked topics or identify truths before they are socially accepted. Contributors benefit more from maintaining acceptable pages than from pushing knowledge forward.

FAQ

Why use a vested ICO instead of an immediate unlock sale?
A vesting schedule can better align token access with product milestones, contributor growth, and long-term governance participation.
How does Acuity differ from a normal wiki?
Acuity emphasizes verifiable provenance, public governance, contributor incentives, and open protocol infrastructure rather than a single centralized platform.
What should be disclosed before a launch?
Token supply, lockups, governance rights, treasury controls, jurisdictional restrictions, and technology risks should all be communicated clearly before participation.

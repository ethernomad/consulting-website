question = "Which AI regulations apply to my organisation?"
answer = "It depends on three factors: <strong>geography</strong> (where you operate and where your users are), <strong>sector</strong> (healthcare, finance, education, and public sector face additional obligations), and <strong>customer type</strong> (B2B enterprises increasingly require SOC 2 as a contractual condition). A structured regulatory exposure assessment maps every applicable framework to your specific context."

[[extra.faq_items]]
question = "Do I need to comply with all five frameworks?"
answer = "Probably not all five, but most organisations are in scope of at least two or three. A US-based B2B SaaS company likely needs NIST AI RMF alignment and SOC 2. If it also has EU customers, the EU AI Act applies. If it handles health data, HIPAA applies. If it operates in Vietnam or serves Vietnamese users, VN AI Law applies. The key is identifying which frameworks apply to <strong>your</strong> specific situation \u2014 then building a unified governance program that addresses them efficiently."

[[extra.faq_items]]
question = "How do these frameworks overlap?"
answer = "Substantially. NIST AI RMF\u2019s four functions provide an operational methodology that satisfies requirements across EU AI Act (risk management, documentation, monitoring), SOC 2 (control design and evidence), and HIPAA (risk analysis and safeguards). Vietnam\u2019s risk-based framework mirrors the EU approach. Work done for one framework directly contributes to readiness for others. A unified governance program is more efficient and cost-effective than managing each framework independently."

[[extra.faq_items]]
question = "What is the most urgent deadline in 2026?"
answer = "<strong>2 August 2026</strong> \u2014 the EU AI Act\u2019s enforcement date for high-risk AI obligations. This is the most pressing deadline for any organisation whose AI systems affect EU citizens. Penalties reach \u20AC35 million or 7% of global annual turnover. For organisations not subject to the EU AI Act, the VN AI Law compliance deadline of March 2027 and US state-level AI legislation (45 states have introduced bills) represent the next wave of regulatory pressure."

[[extra.faq_items]]
question = "Can I use NIST AI RMF instead of the EU AI Act?"
answer = "No \u2014 the EU AI Act is binding regulation; NIST AI RMF is voluntary guidance. However, the AI RMF can serve as a practical implementation methodology for meeting EU AI Act requirements. The European Commission has acknowledged the AI RMF as a useful reference. Many organisations use the AI RMF as their operational framework while addressing EU AI Act-specific obligations (conformity assessment, CE marking, FRIA) as a compliance overlay."

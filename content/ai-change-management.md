[[extra.faq_items]]
question = "How is change management different from training?"
answer = "Training builds capability — it teaches someone how to use a tool. Change management builds willingness and habit — it addresses fear, uncertainty, workflow disruption, and the organisational dynamics that prevent adoption. Both are necessary, but they serve different purposes. We often deliver change management as a complement to technical training, ensuring the skills taught in the classroom actually stick in daily work."

[[extra.faq_items]]
question = "When should we start adoption planning?"
answer = "As early as possible. Ideal timing is during the AI strategy or pilot phase — before the system is built. This allows the adoption plan to influence system design (workflows, UX, rollout sequencing) rather than retrofitting adoption after launch. For existing systems that have already stalled, we can do a rapid adoption assessment and design a recovery plan within 2–3 weeks."

[[extra.faq_items]]
question = "How do you handle resistance from teams?"
answer = "Resistance is almost always a signal, not a problem. It usually means someone's concerns haven't been heard, their workflow hasn't been considered, or they lack the support to make the transition. We surface resistance early through stakeholder interviews and pulse surveys, address the underlying causes directly, and work with champions to turn skeptics into advocates. Most resistance dissolves when people feel heard and equipped."

[[extra.faq_items]]
question = "How long does an adoption programme run?"
answer = "A typical engagement spans 8–16 weeks: 2–4 weeks of assessment and planning, 2–4 weeks of preparation and workshop delivery, and 4–8 weeks of post-launch office hours and reinforcement. We structure the programme so internal champions can sustain adoption independently after the engagement ends. For smaller rollouts, we offer a scaled-down 4–6 week programme."

[[extra.faq_items]]
question = "Do you work with existing AI systems that aren't being adopted?"
answer = "Yes. We frequently help organisations recover stalled AI investments. We do a rapid adoption diagnostic to understand why the system isn't being used, identify the blockers, and design a targeted intervention — which might include refresher workshops, improved documentation, champion reactivation, workflow adjustments, or leadership alignment sessions."

[[extra.faq_items]]
question = "How do you measure adoption success?"
answer = "We track a combination of leading and lagging indicators: active usage rates, feature adoption depth, task completion efficiency, user satisfaction (via pulse surveys), support ticket volume, and business impact metrics tied to the system's original ROI case. We establish baseline measurements before launch and report progress weekly during the engagement, then transition reporting to your internal team."
